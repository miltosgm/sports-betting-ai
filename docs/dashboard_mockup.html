What is Expected Value?
3 min read
▼
The mathematical foundation of profitable betting - why a losing bet can still be 'right'.
Math Prediction is the average outcome if you made the same bet thousands of times. A bet with good value means that, on average, you profit. Example: if a coin flip pays €2 on heads but costs €1 to play, the EV is +€0.50 per flip. In sports betting, we look for bets where the bookmaker's implied probability is lower than the true probability. If Man City truly win 65% of the time but the bookmaker prices them at 60% implied, that's a good value bet - even if City lose tonight.

What is Beat the Market (Market Beat)?
4 min read
▼
The industry gold standard for proving your model has a real AI Advantage - without waiting 1,000 bets.
Beat the Market measures whether you beat the market. When you place a bet at 9AM and the odds shorten to a lower number by kick-off, you 'beat the closing line'. Sharp bookmakers like Pinnacle have the world's best odds compilers. When they move a line, it reflects new information. If you consistently get in before they move, you're ahead of the market. Example: We post Arsenal Away Win @ 1.57. By kick-off, Pinnacle closes at 1.45. We beat the closing line by +8.3% - proof of AI Advantage, regardless of the match result. KickLab tracks your average Market Beat in the Analytics tab.

How optimal bet sizing formula Works
5 min read
▼
The optimal staking formula used by professional gamblers and Wall Street quants alike.
optimal bet sizing formula tells you exactly what percentage of your bankroll to bet, given your AI Advantage and the odds. Formula: Stake % = AI Advantage / (Odds - 1). Example: AI Advantage = 10%, Odds = 2.00 → Stake = 10% / (2.00 - 1) = 10%. On a €10,000 bankroll, that's €1,000. But betting full size is aggressive - one bad run wipes you out. Most pros use Safe bet size (2.5% here = €250). KickLab uses Safe bet size for all stake recommendations. This keeps you in the game through rough patches while still compounding your AI Advantage.

Understanding Rough Patches & Losing Streaks
4 min read
▼
Why you can do everything right and still lose for weeks - and why that's completely fine.
Even with a 77% win rate, losing streaks happen. At 77% win rate, the probability of losing 3 in a row is 1.2%. Sounds rare - but across 100 betting days, you'll hit it several times. The mistake most bettors make: quitting after a bad week, just before the model's AI Advantage reasserts. Rough patches are the short-term noise. your expected results are the long-term signal. Professional bettors track expected results, not just wins. KickLab's Analytics tab shows your actual P/L vs Math Prediction curve - when they diverge, it's a rough patch, not model failure. Stay the course.

Value Betting vs Tips Services
3 min read
▼
Why following a tipster is fundamentally different from systematic value betting.
Traditional tips services sell picks - you trust their judgement on individual games. Value betting is different: it's a systematic process of finding mispricings between the true probability and the bookmaker's odds. Tips services: rely on expert opinion, no statistical framework, results often not independently verified. Value betting: mathematical framework, every pick has a calculated AI Advantage, verifiable via Market Beat, works over large samples. KickLab combines both: ML-driven predictions (systematic) with human-readable reasoning (why this pick). You always know the AI Advantage before you bet.

Building Your Betting Bankroll
5 min read
▼
How to structure your bankroll, how much to start with, and how to grow it systematically.
Start with an amount you're comfortable losing entirely - the model has an AI Advantage, but rough patches mean short-term losses are real. Recommended starting bankroll: €500–€2,000 for beginners. Spread across 3–4 bookmakers (max €500 per bookie to avoid restrictions). Use Safe bet size staking - never bet more than 3% of bankroll on a single pick. Withdraw profits quarterly, reinvest 50% to compound growth. At 2.5% Return and Safe bet size, a €1,000 bankroll betting 100 picks/month grows to ~€1,340 in 3 months. Discipline beats size. A small bankroll with strict staking outperforms a large bankroll with reckless betting every time.

📊 How we calculate these numbers
🤖 Kick Lab AI %
Our AI model (trained on 16,000+ Premier League games) analyses travel distance, team form, injuries, home advantage and 48 other factors to calculate the true probability of this outcome happening.
🏦 Bookmaker %
Converted directly from the bookmaker odds. Odds of 3.60 = 1 ÷ 3.60 = 27.8%. This is what the bookmaker thinks the probability is — including their profit margin.
🔥 Our Edge %
The difference between our AI probability and the bookmaker's implied probability. A positive number means we think the outcome is more likely than the bookmaker does — that gap is where the value is. Bigger gap = stronger bet.

Kick Lab AI says 39.8% · Bookmaker says 28% · Our Edge = +11.8%
The bookmaker underpriced this outcome — that is the bet.
